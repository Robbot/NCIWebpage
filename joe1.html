Sterilising

First things first... Sterlise, Sterlise, Sterlise !

The most important thing to do when making homebrew is to sterise ALL equipment and materials which will come into to contact with your alcohol. Yeast is an active ingredient and can cause bacteria to multiply. This is a good thing because it develops the alcohol and flavours, but it can also multiply bad bacteria. So, sterlising must be done to prevent this. Its not as much fun as brewing or sampling the outcome :)... But once this process is done you can have peace of mind that your product is safe to drink and does not contain any nasty bugs !!!

India Pale Ale

IPA (India Pale Ale) is an ale which originates from pale malt, it is often quite bitter and has an aquired taste. This variation of ale has an emphasis on using hops and hence there is a very hoppy taste from IPA. It is often double hopped to emphasise the hoppiness

    Ingredients
    50g/1.75 oz Golding hops
    100g / 3.5 oz flaked maize
    900g / 2 lb diasitic malt syrup
    500g / 1 lb 2oz light brown sugar
    100g/3.5 oz crystal malt grains
    100g/3.5 oz crystal flaked rice
    9 litres / 2 gallons [2.5 US gallons] hard water
    5ml / 1 teaspoon beer yeast granules

    Procedure: Boil an handful of hops along with the maize in half the water for 45 minutes. Then strain them out with a nylon bag and press them until dry. Stir the malt grains into the wort, cover and leave to cool. Add the rest of the cold water and stir in the yeast. Leave to ferment @ 17 degrees.

    The next day, skim of the dirty froth from the top and discard, then stir the wort. Repeat this process the following day. Then wet the remaining hops and add to the wort. Again leave to ferment, this time allow the beer to finish. When the fermentation is complete, move to a cool place, this will wllow for the the ber to clear. Once the beer has cleared, syphon into bottles, add about 5mls od sugar to every 1.2 litres. Seal the bottles for at least 4 weeks before opening on a nice sunny day !
